The Concept

Behaviour is the product

The Problem
Industrial safety training is delivered through videos, slideshows, and static procedures. Trainees pass a test. They enter a real hazard and freeze — because knowing the procedure and executing it under pressure are different skills.
The Approach
HazardXR places the trainee inside the hazard in their actual physical environment using MR passthrough. The real room is the training environment. No environment reskin. No suspension of disbelief. The hazard appears where they work.
The Scoring
Three competency signals are captured per attempt: selection accuracy (did they identify the right hazard?), action sequence (did they respond in the right order?), and response time (how fast under pressure?).
The Routing
Scores determine the next scenario. Perform above threshold — advance to a harder variant. Perform below — routed to a corrective scenario targeting the specific failure mode. Competency-gated, not time-gated.

The Five-Step Demo Flow

01
Scene Entry
Trainee enters their physical space wearing Quest 3. MR passthrough activates. The room anchors via OVRSceneManager — furniture, walls, and floor are detected. A fire hazard spawns in the environment.
02
Hazard Recognition
Trainee must identify the hazard class before acting. Wrong identification triggers a corrective branch.
Signal: Selection Accuracy
03
Extinguisher Selection
Three extinguisher types available. Correct selection required — wrong choice fails the scenario. Matches NFPA 10 extinguisher classification.
Signal: Selection Accuracy
04
PASS Technique
Pull — Aim — Squeeze — Sweep. Each step must be executed in sequence and within spatial tolerance. Misordering is logged and scored.
Signal: Action Sequence · Response Time
05
Outcome Scoring & Routing
Composite score calculated: ≥80% overall with ≥60% on Execution advances. Below threshold routes to a corrective scenario targeting the specific failure. Score logged locally in JSON for E01.
Signal: Composite Score → Next Scenario

Research

The PhD research question

How do competency signals — selection accuracy, action sequence, and response time — determine next scenario routing in spatial simulation for industrial safety training?
HazardXR is the concrete research vehicle for this question. The simulation is not incidental to the research — it is the research artefact. Every interaction in the experience generates the data that the research question is built around.

The theoretical umbrella is CASE — Context-Aware Spatial Environments — an emerging framework for spatial systems that adapt to learner behaviour in real-time. "Competency-gated" is a coined distinction: unlike "competency-based" systems that require demonstration before progression, competency-gated systems use live performance signals to route within an experience.
Priority supervisory target: Spatial Dynamics + Howest + Ghent/imec · Belgium

What This Is Not

Not Competency-Based
Competency-based systems gate progression on demonstrated achievement before moving forward. HazardXR routes within the experience based on live performance — corrective or advanced — without stopping the simulation.
Not Adaptive Learning (generic)
Generic adaptive learning adjusts content difficulty. HazardXR routes to specific scenario variants targeting specific failure modes — the routing logic is domain-specific and grounded in NFPA/OSHA standards.

The Build

Two modes. One environment.

Primary Mode
MR Passthrough
The trainee's real room is the training environment. Hazards spawn into actual space. Scene understanding via OVRSceneManager detects surfaces and anchors virtual objects to real geometry.
Secondary Mode
Full VR
A fully rendered 3D environment for contexts where the real room isn't available or appropriate. Same interaction model, same scoring — different immersion mode.
