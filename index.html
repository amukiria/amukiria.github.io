Safety training that changes behaviour, not just awareness

HazardXR is a modular safety simulation family for high-risk training environments. Each episode teaches one critical safety behaviour through immersive, consequence-driven experience — not passive instruction.

The Family

Three apps. One family.
Each medium earns its place.

The medium is not a marketing choice — it is a scenario design decision. HazardXR splits by what the scenario demands, not what is convenient to build.

Active Build
HazardMR

Your environment becomes the workplace. Hazards, equipment, and consequence are overlaid on your real space. Passthrough MR — the danger feels present because your surroundings are.

8 Episodes MR Passthrough Quest 3
Planned
HazardVR

Scenarios where the physical environment itself is the hazard. Heights. Enclosed spaces. Large-scale disaster. Full immersion is not a preference — it is the only way to make these scenarios work.

3 Episodes Full VR Quest 3
Future
HazardAR

Mobile and tablet AR — a safety manager walks the factory floor and sees hazard overlays in real time. No headset. No setup. The lowest barrier to entry in the family and the widest reach. Episodes TBD.

In planning
Episodes

Failures that cannot happen twice.

Each episode is built from a documented safety failure. No episode starts until the one before it ships. The shell manages session, progression, and scoring — episodes load as self-contained modules on top of it.

About

Built by Albert Mukiria

HazardXR is designed and built by Albert Mukiria — Senior Product Manager and XR developer with hands-on Unity and Quest 3 capability. The series demonstrates what becomes possible when product strategy meets immersive technology in high-stakes training contexts.

Every decision in HazardXR is deliberate — the medium split between HazardMR and HazardVR, the episode order, the standards grounding, the backend architecture. The product is not a prototype. It is a considered product strategy executed from first principles.

The goal is not novelty. The goal is behaviour change at the moment that matters.
